Let $(G, *, e)$ be a group and let $a \in G$.
The function
\begin{align*}
L_a: G &\rightarrow G \\
L_a(x) &= a * x
\end{align*}
is a bijection.
In particular, every row and every column
of a group table is a permutation of $G$.
$L_a$ is sometimes called the \defone{left translation map} by $a$.
